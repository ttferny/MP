Key insight

When should you use each?

~all — soft fail
  • Setting up SPF for the first time
  • Migrating to a new mail provider
  • Not yet sure all your mail sources are listed
  • Want to monitor without risking broken delivery
Treat ~all as temporary. Move to -all once you confirm your record is complete.
-all — hard fail
  • Your SPF record is tested and confirmed correct
  • You want maximum protection against spoofing
  • Your domain is a target for phishing or BEC attacks
  • You have DMARC set to quarantine or reject
-all is the recommended end state for any domain that sends email.
